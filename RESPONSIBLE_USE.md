# Responsible Use Policy

VoiceLace is a local-first audiobook and voice production workstation. It can
combine speech recognition, text parsing, text-to-speech, voice cloning, music
generation, and media export. These capabilities are useful for creative and
accessibility workflows, but they can also harm people when used without
permission or disclosure.

Use VoiceLace only when you have the rights, consent, and legal basis needed
for the material you process or publish.

## Allowed and Encouraged Uses

- Producing audiobooks, podcasts, demos, accessibility audio, localization, or
  personal creative projects from material you own or are authorized to use.
- Cloning or synthesizing a voice with the speaker's informed consent.
- Creating synthetic narration for fictional characters where no real person is
  impersonated in a misleading way.
- Testing models locally with private data that you are allowed to process.
- Clearly labeling synthetic or AI-assisted media when context requires it.

## Prohibited Uses

- Cloning, imitating, or synthesizing a real person's voice without consent
  when the output could be mistaken for that person.
- Creating deceptive endorsements, fraud, scams, harassment, impersonation, or
  misinformation.
- Removing, hiding, or falsifying provenance, consent records, copyright
  notices, or required synthetic-media disclosures.
- Processing private, confidential, biometric, copyrighted, or personal data
  without the necessary rights or legal basis.
- Using outputs to evade platform policies, payment rules, identity checks, or
  legal obligations.

## User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for:

- Verifying the license and permitted use of each model, checkpoint, dataset,
  sample, dependency, and asset.
- Obtaining consent for voice cloning, speaker imitation, and biometric voice
  processing.
- Respecting copyright, neighboring rights, privacy, publicity, personality,
  trademark, and consumer-protection laws.
- Reviewing generated content before publication.
- Disclosing synthetic or cloned media where required by law, platform policy,
  contract, or audience expectations.
- Keeping records of consent, source provenance, and model licenses for
  commercial or public releases.
